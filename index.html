General Game Design Tips

Make It Responsive
- Don't let your controls, gameplay, user interface, and other game elements feel stiff or even unfair.

Players will feel frsutrated if they clicked the jump button and missed a pixel of the collision box, hence not registering the jump.

Learn Color Theory
- Some colors mix better than others, take advantage of that and learn color theory.

Altough color preference is subjective, some color themes don't generally work together.

Make It Unique
- Everyone is different; utilize your uniqueness as a source of creative ideas.

Indie games tend to have more charm than AAA ones because indie developers can construct appeal based on their own individual uniqueness any the want to.

Know Your Audience
- Adapt your design based on our target audience's expectations.

Hack-and-Slash games shouldn't have intrusions like long tutorials or slow animations. Players will expect fast paced gameplay if they chose to play a hack-and-slash game.

Keep A Theme
- Consider what our game will compose of, then build a theme off of it.

An immersive game should avoid interacting with players outside of the game world. An example is to apply tutorials as part of the story, instead of shoving it infront of players' faces.

Keep It Engaging
- Keep the gameplay fresh and active, prevent it from becoming dull.

Repetitive gameplay can turn stale; so be sure to introduce new mechanics to your game every now and then, even small but frequent changes and additions can keep the gamepla fresh.

Avoid Long Texts
- A niche tip but, keep your texts concise and to the point.

Unless it's supposed to be detailed, avoid using lengthy text that uses unnecessary words. Also adding to the previous advice, avoid using jargon that your target audience won't understand.
